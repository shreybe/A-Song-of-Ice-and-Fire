Project 3 · NASA MODIS / FIRMS

Where are satellite-detected fire hotspots concentrated—and how bright are they?

MODIS thermal anomalies mark likely fires and other heat sources. This prototype maps each detection as a small spark (ZipDecode-style luminosity on a dark field), supports dynamic filtering and brushing, and reveals details on demand when you click a spark.

Exploratory views

Data transformation: Points follow the NASA FIRMS MODIS_NRT CSV schema (brightness Kelvin, FRP in MW, acquisition date/time, Terra/Aqua, confidence). Region splits longitude into West / Central / East for coarse geography. The bundled CSV is a demo ensemble for layout and interaction testing; replace it with a FIRMS export using scripts/fetch_firms_modis.py and your free MAP_KEY for live MODIS.

Checkpoint write-up (draft)

Rationale

Sparks use position for geography and luminosity for intensity (radius scales with FRP, warm color for brightness). Dynamic queries (date, confidence, day/night) narrow the population; brushing on FRP vs brightness supports linked selection like lecture linked views. Pan/zoom assists spatial comparison without cluttering static ink.

Alternatives considered

  • Choropleth by county—obscures point-level MODIS resolution; chose dot marks.
  • Constant-size dots—understates high-FRP events; chose sqrt scale on FRP.
